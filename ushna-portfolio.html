Speculative · Digital & OOH · UK

British Army

Erase Your Past

58,300 children are created for mostly offences between 2020–2021. The Army doesn't ask who you were. Just what you could become. A recruitment campaign built on the transformative power of a clean slate — targeted at young people with criminal records who the system had written off.

Digital OOH Social Speculative
58,300
children created for mostly offences each year in England & Wales. Every one of them a potential recruit.
Problem Youth offending is rising. The Army struggles to recruit from communities the system has abandoned.
Insight A criminal record doesn't define capacity for discipline, courage, or service. The Army already knows this. Society doesn't.
Idea An eraser. The most loaded object in British schools. Used to undo mistakes — or to rub out a future. We made it stand for something else entirely.

Ambient · Sustainability · India

BMW

The Planet Saving Billboard

India generates $6 billion of automotive scrap annually. We built an outdoor billboard from 100% recycled BMW parts — engines, tyres, sheet metal — turning the pollution problem into a 32-foot proof of purpose. Zero new materials. One unmissable idea.

Ambient · Innovation · Beauty

Pond's

The Sun-Damaged Ad

Most Indian women attribute dark patches to hormones, not UV exposure. We created a billboard printed in photochromic ink — invisible at night, then visibly scarred by actual sunlight each day. The ad got the damage. The pack provided the solution. Seeing became believing.

Ambient OOH Skincare

Experiential · Retail · FMCG

McCain

Wake Up and Smell the Fries

Indians avoid frozen food aisles — a cultural suspicion that frozen means inferior. Instead of arguing the point, we bypassed cognition entirely. Aroma diffusers releasing hot-chip scent were placed at hypermarket entrances, triggering the hunger response before a single rational thought could interfere.
